Happiness Score v.s Log GDP per Capita

If we compare life happiness reports from around the world at any given point in time, we can see that most countries with higher average national incomes tend to have higher average life happiness scores. In other words: People in richer countries tend to report higher life satisfaction than people in poorer countries. The scatter plot shows this. Each dot in the graph represents one country. The vertical position of the dots shows Happiness scores; while the horizontal position shows Log GDP per capita based on purchasing power parity.
